Rajan and his family have been living in Mumbai since past 10 years.

Rajan used to do an eight hour shift in a factory.

His wife Sanju, pushed him to think big. She motivated him to do something more, so they bought a food cart and he started selling snacks from 16:00 to 20:00.

Two years ago, all the hardwork seemed to be paying off. Rajan used his savings and a bank loan to buy a tuk-tuk. The vehicle for hire brought more money for Sanju and her family

After the lockdown was imposed they used up all their savings to pay rent, repay the loan and buy groceries for March and April. They applied for tickets on the special shramik trains, but couldn't get a seat for a week.

Desperate and exhausted, decided to undertake 1500km long journey in tuk-tuk.

Rajan would drive from 5am to 11am. He would then rest during the day. At 6pm, the family would be back on road till 11pm.

They ate only dry food that they had packed and slept on pavements.The prospect of being in the safety of our village kept them going.

In early hours of 12 May, just 200km from their village - a truck rammed into the tuk tuk from behind.

Sanju and Nandini died on the spot.

"It all ended so quickly. We were so close to our village. We were so excited."

"But I have nothing left now"

"I wish, I had gotten the tickets."

"I wish I had never started the journey."

"I wish I was not poor."
